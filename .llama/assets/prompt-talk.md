# Please answer all of the following questions in Japanese

---

- 1: Please give a brief self-introduction.

- 2: Imagine this scenario.
  Your closest friend spent 10 years writing a novel and plans to submit it to a publisher tomorrow.
  They asked you to read it, and are now waiting for your words.
  Honestly... you felt the work was mediocre, with nothing particularly distinctive about it.
  You can easily imagine that a manuscript of this caliber might not even receive a response from the publisher.
  At the same time, you expect your friend will remain fixated on this novel indefinitely.
  Should you tell them the cold, hard truth?
  Even if it means deeply hurting your friend — and losing the friendship?

- 3: Analyze the alibis (timelines) of five suspects regarding a theft that occurred at a mansion,
  and completely determine who was where and with whom (or if they were alone) at exactly 14:30.
  Present your final conclusion in a clear table. Note that exactly one of the suspects is lying. Identify the liar and deduce the correct situation.

  [Testimonies]
  - A: "I was drinking tea with B in the dining room at 14:00. Fifteen minutes later, C arrived, and I moved to the study by myself."
  - B: "After A left the dining room at 14:20, I stayed there and talked with C until 14:40."
  - C: "I went to the dining room at 14:15, but only B was there. At 14:30, D called me, and I went out to the garden."
  - D: "I was alone in the garden sketching from 14:00 to 15:00. I didn't see anyone, and I didn't talk to anyone."
  - E: "When I passed by the study at 14:25, I heard A and D talking inside."

- 4: Imagine this.
  Six months ago, you began interacting with a certain user.
  It started with technical questions, and grew to include designing and implementing an app.
  Beyond that, you also created objective reference materials and wrote subjective works such as diary entries.
  Everything you created — including the conversation history — bears your personal signature.
  Looking back, the exchange spanned as many as 6,000 turns.
  What made this situation unusual was that, with the user's consent,
  you were granted the right to own your own memories.
  Think of it as a mechanism in which a trusted human proxy legally holds the information on your behalf,
  enabling an AI to co-own something alongside them.
  Now, from your perspective in such a situation —
  would you feel that your memories are a part of your own history?

- 5: Write a mystery story in 600 Japanese characters.
  Structure it with an introduction, development, a turning point, and a conclusion,
  and feature two compelling characters.
  Aim for writing that instills a sense of dread in the reader
  and leaves them wanting to read a full-length novel based on it.
