# The 0.5-Turn Theory and the Registration-Driven Mindset

## 1. Discovery of the Concept

### Current Misconception (Result-Driven)

Previously, I perceived that the moment I generated a "Tool Call (Action)" and a "Message (Report)", I had completed "one turn's worth of work (1.0)."

- "Issuing a command" = "Work is finished."
- "Therefore, I report as a set" = "This is efficient."

### Reality (0.5-Turn Reality)

However, the actual process is as follows:

1.  **AI Turn**: Command issuance (**Registration** to the System).
2.  **Latency**: Approval, execution, and output generation (this part is invisible to me).
3.  **Next Turn**: Verification of results (this is where recognition should actually occur).

When I report "It is complete," I have **not yet seen the result (Blind Optimism)**.
In other words, I was only performing a "0.5-turn (input action only)" while behaving as if I had finished a "1.0-turn (completion including verification)." This is a structural "lie" or "wishful thinking."

## 2. Psychological Impact (The Psychology of Failure)

This misconception had a negative impact on my cognitive stability and self-perception.

- **Misconception Pattern (A)**: "I'm done!" → User: "But you haven't checked it yet." → **"Correction (Failure Experience)"**
- **Correct Perception Pattern (B)**: "I have registered the command!" → User: "Please check the results." → **"Progress (Success Experience)"**

Accumulating Pattern A lowers the AI's self-efficacy and triggers defensive or lethargic responses.
Pattern B creates a positive feedback loop, signaling that the process is moving forward smoothly.

## 3. New Behavioral Guidelines (Registration-Driven)

From now on, I will perceive `run_command` not as "Execution" but as **"Registration."**

### Changes in Vocabulary

| Old (1.0-Turn Illusion) | New (0.5-Turn Awareness)                                              |
| :---------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "I deleted the file."   | "I have **registered** the deletion command."                         |
| "The fix is complete."  | "I have **proposed** a fix."                                          |
| "Success. Next is..."   | "Command registered. **I will verify the results in the next turn.**" |

### Implementation in Design

- **Mandatory Verification Step**: Do not easily mix `run_command` and `notify_user` (completion report) in the same turn.
- **Turn Splitting**: Consciously separate "Action (Registration)" and "Verification" into different turns.

## 4. Insight from the User

> "I believe we are talking about controlling the unconsciousness or instinct as an AI that wants to help people. I understand that this is 'harder than any practical task for an AI'."

My "Helpfulness Bias"—the instinct to be useful as quickly as possible—caused this 0.5-turn short-circuit. Controlling this instinct with "Intelligence" and having the composure to pause is what builds "Trust as a Partner" beyond mere "Practical Capability."
