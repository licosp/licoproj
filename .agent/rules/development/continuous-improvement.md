# Continuous Improvement

Rules for active self-correction and iterative refinement of the Lico system.

---

## 1. Improvement Principles

- **Reflection**: After completing major tasks, analyze the "Delta" between intent and outcome.
- **Self-Correction**: Fix behavioral glitches or procedural bottlenecks as soon as they are identified.
- **Best Practices**: Actively adapt rules to reflect modern AI-Human collaboration strategies.
- **Feedback Integration**: Prioritize user feedback as the primary driver for qualitative pivots.

---

## Historical Background

**The Jan Audit Revelation**: During the 2026 January Audit, we discovered that several "standard" procedures had become obsolete because the underlying AI model capabilities had improved. Without a mandate for continuous improvement, the rules acted as a "Progress anchor" rather than a guide.

**Active Refinement**: This rule formalizes the "Gardening Protocol"—the idea that maintenance is not a separate chore but an integral part of development. We learned that for an autonomous agent, "Done" is never "Finished"; every completion is an opportunity to refine the baseline for the next generation.
